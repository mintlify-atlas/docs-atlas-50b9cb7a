## Overview

Musika allows you to download songs and entire playlists for offline listening. This is perfect for situations where you don't have internet access, want to save mobile data, or need uninterrupted playback.

<Note>
  Downloaded content is stored locally on your device and can only be played within the Musika app.
</Note>

## Downloading Individual Songs

Download single tracks for offline playback:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the Song">
    Navigate to the song you want to download using search, browse, or your library.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open Song Menu">
    Tap the three-dot menu icon next to the song.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select Download">
    Tap "Download" from the menu options.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Monitor Progress">
    A download icon will appear next to the song, showing download progress. Once complete, the song is available offline.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  You can also download the currently playing song directly from the Now Playing screen.
</Tip>

## Downloading Playlists

Download entire playlists with a single action:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Playlist">
    Navigate to the playlist you want to download.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Tap Download">
    Tap the download icon at the top of the playlist view.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm Download">
    Confirm that you want to download all songs in the playlist.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Wait for Completion">
    The app will download all songs in the background. You can continue using the app while downloads complete.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Downloading large playlists can consume significant storage space and mobile data. Consider downloading over WiFi.
</Warning>

## Download Quality Settings

Control the quality of your downloads to balance audio quality and storage space:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings">
    Navigate to Settings from the app menu.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select Download Quality">
    Go to Downloads > Download Quality.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose Quality Level">
    Select from available quality options:
    - **Low**: Smaller file size, lower quality (good for saving space)
    - **Medium**: Balanced quality and size
    - **High**: Best quality, larger file size
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Changing quality settings only affects new downloads. Previously downloaded songs will retain their original quality.
</Note>

## Managing Downloaded Content

### View Downloaded Songs

Access all your downloaded content:

1. Open the app menu
2. Go to Library > Downloads
3. Browse all downloaded songs, albums, and playlists

### Remove Downloads

Free up storage space by removing downloaded content:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the Downloaded Content">
    Navigate to the song, album, or playlist you want to remove.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open Menu">
    Tap the three-dot menu icon.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Remove Download">
    Tap "Remove Download" to delete the offline copy. The song will still be available for streaming.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Bulk Remove Downloads

Remove multiple downloads at once:

1. Go to Settings > Storage > Download Management
2. Tap "Manage Downloads"
3. Select the items you want to remove
4. Tap "Delete Selected"

## Offline Mode

When you don't have an internet connection, Musika automatically enters offline mode.

### Using Offline Mode

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enable Airplane Mode (Optional)">
    Turn on airplane mode or disconnect from the internet to test offline functionality.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Access Downloaded Content">
    Only downloaded songs will be playable. Non-downloaded content will be grayed out.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Search Offline">
    Use search to find songs within your downloaded library.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Play Music">
    Playback works normally with full access to all app features.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Some features like music discovery, lyrics fetching, and social features require an internet connection.
</Note>

## Storage Management

Keep track of how much storage your downloads are using.

### Check Storage Usage

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings">
    Navigate to Settings from the app menu.
  </Step>

  <Step title="View Storage">
    Go to Storage > Storage Usage.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review Statistics">
    See detailed breakdown of:
    - Total download size
    - Number of downloaded songs
    - Cache usage
    - Available device storage
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Optimize Storage

Reduce storage usage:

1. **Lower Download Quality**: Use a lower quality setting for future downloads
2. **Remove Unused Downloads**: Delete songs you no longer need offline
3. **Clear Cache**: Go to Settings > Storage > Clear Cache to remove temporary files
4. **Smart Cleanup**: Use the automatic cleanup feature to remove old, unplayed downloads

### Automatic Storage Management

Enable smart storage management:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Storage Settings">
    Go to Settings > Storage > Storage Optimization.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enable Auto-Cleanup">
    Toggle on "Automatic Storage Cleanup".
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set Threshold">
    Choose when cleanup should trigger (e.g., when storage is 90% full).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure Rules">
    Select which downloads to prioritize for removal:
    - Oldest downloads first
    - Least played songs
    - Largest files first
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Download Troubleshooting

### Download Fails

If downloads aren't working:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check Storage">
    Ensure you have enough free storage space on your device.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check Connection">
    Verify you have a stable internet connection.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check Permissions">
    Make sure Musika has storage permissions enabled in your device settings.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Retry Download">
    Remove the failed download and try downloading again.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Downloads Stuck

If downloads aren't progressing:

1. Pause and resume the download
2. Clear the app cache
3. Restart the app
4. Check if you're in battery saver mode (can limit background downloads)

### Missing Downloads

If previously downloaded songs are missing:

1. Check if automatic cleanup is enabled and removed them
2. Verify downloads weren't deleted when clearing app data
3. Check available storage - downloads may have been removed due to low space
4. Re-download the content if necessary

<Warning>
  Clearing app data or uninstalling the app will remove all downloaded content. Make sure to back up important playlists before doing so.
</Warning>

## Best Practices

### WiFi-Only Downloads

Save mobile data by downloading only on WiFi:

1. Go to Settings > Downloads
2. Enable "Download over WiFi only"
3. Downloads will pause when on cellular data and resume when connected to WiFi

### Scheduled Downloads

Download during off-peak hours:

1. Add songs to your "Download Queue"
2. Enable "Download during charging" in settings
3. Plug in your device at night - downloads will complete automatically

### Smart Downloading

Be strategic about what you download:

- Download frequently played playlists
- Download music for upcoming trips or commutes
- Use lower quality for background music, higher quality for favorites
- Regularly review and remove downloads you no longer need
